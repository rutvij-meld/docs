---
title: "Transaction Statuses"
---

Fiat transactions move through several statuses during their lifecycle. This page lists every status you might see on a Meld fiat transaction and is intended for developers integrating card or ACH payments. Statuses are either **TEMPORARY** (the transaction may still change) or **TERMINAL** (the transaction will not change again).

The most common lifecycle of a successful transaction is `PENDING` → `SETTLING` → `SETTLED`. `PENDING` means the user has initiated a payment, `SETTLING` means the payment has been submitted, and `SETTLED` means the payment has processed successfully. These three, plus `FAILED`, are the four most common and most important transaction statuses.

<Note>
For the ACH-specific lifecycle and statuses, see [ACH Transaction Statuses](/docs/payments-stack/fiat-payment-quickstart/making-ach-payments/ach-transaction-statuses).
</Note>

| Status                  | Description                                                                                                                                                  | State     |
| :---------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :-------- |
| `PENDING_CREATED`       | The transaction has been created in Meld's system but has not yet been processed by the service provider.                                                    | TEMPORARY |
| `PENDING`               | The transaction has been created in Meld's system and submitted to the service provider for review. Once verified, the funds will be sent to the recipient.   | TEMPORARY |
| `AUTHORIZED`            | The transaction has been approved by the bank that issues the customer's payment card.                                                                       | TEMPORARY |
| `AUTHORIZATION_EXPIRED` | The transaction was authorized but never submitted for capture. Authorizations typically expire approximately 30 days after the initial authorization.        | TEMPORARY |
| `SETTLING`              | Fiat funds are being moved from your customer's bank through your account.                                                                                    | TEMPORARY |
| `SETTLED`               | The transaction has been approved and the customer's fiat funds have successfully settled.                                                                    | TERMINAL  |
| `DISPUTED`              | The account holder whose money was debited has disputed the transaction and it is under review.                                                              | TEMPORARY |
| `REFUNDED`              | A full refund has been successfully issued to your customer.                                                                                                 | TERMINAL  |
| `DECLINED`              | The payment method used for the transaction has been declined. Common card-decline reasons include insufficient funds, an inactive card, or a stolen card.   | TERMINAL  |
| `CANCELLED`             | The transaction has been canceled by the customer or merchant during the payment process. Any settling fiat funds will not be sent for settlement.            | TERMINAL  |
| `FAILED`                | The transaction has failed. See the common failure reasons below.                                                                                            | TERMINAL  |
| `ERROR`                 | An error occurred while processing the transaction.                                                                                                          | TERMINAL  |
| `VOIDED`                | The transaction has been terminated early in the payment lifecycle and no fiat funds have moved.                                                              | TERMINAL  |

## Common reasons a transaction may show `FAILED`

- **Do Not Honor** — the bank is unwilling to accept the transaction.
- **Insufficient Funds** — the account did not have sufficient funds to cover the transaction amount at the time of the transaction.
- **Limit Exceeded** — the attempted transaction exceeds the withdrawal limit of the account.
- **Expired Card** — the card is expired. The customer will need to use a different card.
- **Invalid Card Number** — the customer entered an invalid payment method or made a typo in their card information.
- **Invalid Expiration Date** — the customer entered an invalid expiration date. Have the customer correct their payment information and try again — if the decline persists they will need to contact their bank.
- **Card Issuer Declined CVV** — the customer entered an invalid security code or made a typo. Have the customer try again — if the decline persists they will need to contact their bank.
- **Based on Location** — the bank declined based on location.
- **Bank's Fraud Rules** — the bank's fraud rules blocked the transaction.
