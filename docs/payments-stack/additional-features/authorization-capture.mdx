---
title: "Authorization & Capture"
---

Authorization and capture is a two-step process for completing card payments. This page is for developers who need to reserve funds at order time and charge the customer later — for example, an eCommerce pre-order that ships once inventory arrives.

## How it works

1. **Authorization** — verifies the customer has sufficient funds for the charge but does not actually charge the card. The transaction status moves to `AUTHORIZED`.
2. **Capture** — captures some or all of the authorized amount to finalize the sale. After a successful capture, the transaction status moves from `AUTHORIZED` to `SETTLED`.

<Note>
Authorizations typically expire roughly 30 days after the initial authorization if never captured. After that, the status moves to `AUTHORIZATION_EXPIRED`. See [Transaction Statuses](/docs/payments-stack/fiat-transaction-statuses) for the full lifecycle.
</Note>

## Example use case

An eCommerce order is placed for an item that is not in stock immediately, but will ship shortly (a pre-order). Authorization is initiated at order time. Once the goods are available to ship, the capture request is issued and the card is charged.

<Tip>
Not every service provider supports authorization and capture. See [Supported Service Providers](/docs/payments-stack/fiat-supported-service-providers) to confirm support.
</Tip>
